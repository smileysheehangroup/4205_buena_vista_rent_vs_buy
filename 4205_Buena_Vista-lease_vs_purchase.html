💰 Ancillary Benefits of Ownership

~$50,000/yr
Mortgage Interest Deduction
Yr 1 interest ≈ $357K on $5.436M loan. IRS caps deductibility at $750K acquisition debt — but at the 37% bracket that's still ~$50K in annual federal tax savings on the deductible portion.
$0 on First $250K
Capital Gains Exclusion
Primary residence occupied 2 of last 5 years qualifies for up to $250K gain exclusion (single filer) at sale — a meaningful benefit on a high-appreciation luxury asset.
Future Flexibility
HELOC / Cash-Out Refi
Equity unlocks borrowing power without a taxable sale. If rates drop in 1–3 years, he can refinance to a lower payment. A renter has no equivalent lever.

The Bottom Line: If He Plans to Buy Anyway

Leasing first costs $1,200,000 in unrecoverable rent — roughly 18% of the purchase price — just to defer the decision. During that same year as an owner, he would build meaningful equity, earn appreciation on a $6.795M asset, and access six-figure tax deductions. The lease makes sense only if there is real uncertainty about the property itself or a specific reason to preserve liquidity.
